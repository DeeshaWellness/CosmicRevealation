The Cosmic Revelation of Kalpataru

Verse in Sanskrit:
मया ततमिदं सर्वं जगदव्यक्तमूर्तिना।
मत्स्थानि सर्वभूतानि न चाहं तेष्ववस्थितः।

DeSandhi Version
मया ततम् इदं सर्वं जगत् अव्यक्तमूर्तिना।
मत्स्थानि सर्वभूतानि न च अहं तेषु अवस्थितः।

Verse Source:
Bhagavad Gita, Chapter IX, Verse 4

English Translation:
By Me, in My unmanifested form, this entire universe is pervaded. All beings are in Me, but I am not in them.

Transliteration:
mayā tatam idaṁ sarvaṁ jagad avyakta-mūrtinā
mat-sthāni sarva-bhūtāni na ca ahaṁ teṣv avasthitaḥ

Grammatical Analysis:

  • The words “ततम्” and “अवस्थितः” are both perfect participles indicating a completed action.
  • “मया” and “अव्यक्तमूर्तिना” are in the instrumental case, indicating the means by which the action is performed.
  • “इदं”, “सर्वं”, “जगत्”, “मत्स्थानि”, and “सर्वभूतानि” are all in the nominative case, indicating the subject or object of the sentence.

Phonetic Features:

  • The sandhi (morphophonemic changes) observed are:
    • मया + ततम् = मया ततमिदं
    • जगत् + अव्यक्तमूर्तिना = जगदव्यक्तमूर्तिना
    • न + च = न चाहं
    • तेषु + अवस्थितः = तेष्ववस्थितः

Cultural or Philosophical Significance:
This verse from the Bhagavad Gita emphasizes the omnipresence of the divine consciousness, represented by “Me” or the Self. While the universe is pervaded by this divine consciousness, the consciousness remains transcendental and is not confined or limited by the universe. The verse underscores the non-dual nature of reality, a central tenet in Vedantic philosophy.

The Cosmic Revelation of Kalpataru

In a timeless realm, there was an ethereal tree known as the Kalpataru, the Tree of Life. Its roots spread out infinitely in all directions, and its branches reached out to embrace the cosmos. Every leaf, flower, and fruit on the Kalpataru represented a unique universe, each with its own galaxies, stars, planets, and life forms.

A curious soul named Dev approached the Kalpataru, seeking to understand the mysteries of existence. As he looked at one of the leaves, he was instantly transported into a universe, experiencing its vastness, beauty, and complexities.

After what felt like eons, Dev returned to his original form and found himself still standing before the Kalpataru. A celestial being named Aditi, the guardian of the tree, greeted him.

“Aditi,” Dev exclaimed, “the universes within each leaf are vast and intricate. How can they all exist within this single tree?”

Aditi smiled and replied, “The Kalpataru is a manifestation of the divine consciousness. Just as a single tree holds countless leaves, each representing a universe, the divine consciousness pervades and encompasses all that exists. Nothing stands outside of it.”

“But,” Dev pondered, “if every universe, with its joys, sorrows, creation, and destruction, is within the divine consciousness, how does it remain unaffected?”

Aditi picked a ripe fruit from the tree and handed it to Dev. As he touched it, he felt the vibrations of countless lives and events within that universe. Yet, the fruit itself was whole and unblemished.

Aditi explained, “Just as this fruit contains the essence of an entire universe but remains whole, the divine consciousness, while holding everything within it, remains untouched and unaltered. It is the eternal witness, the ultimate reality.”

Dev gazed at the Kalpataru with newfound reverence. He realized that while the divine consciousness was immanent in everything, it transcended all forms and phenomena. It was both the container and the content, the observer and the observed, but forever unchanging.

With gratitude in his heart, Dev embarked on his cosmic journey, carrying the profound wisdom that nothing is apart from the divine consciousness, and yet it remains ever pure and unaffected.
